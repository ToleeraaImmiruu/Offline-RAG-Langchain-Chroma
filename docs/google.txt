Google LLC is an American multinational technology company focused on internet-related services and products, including search, online advertising, cloud computing, software, hardware, and artificial intelligence.

Overview

Google was founded on September 4, 1998, in Menlo Park, California, by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were PhD students at Stanford University. The company originally started as a search engine and quickly grew into one of the most influential technology companies in the world.

In 2004, Google became a publicly traded company through an initial public offering (IPO). In 2015, Google was reorganized as a wholly owned subsidiary of Alphabet Inc., which became the parent holding company. Google remains Alphabet’s largest subsidiary.

Leadership and Organization

Sundar Pichai became the CEO of Google on October 24, 2015, succeeding Larry Page. In 2019, Pichai also became the CEO of Alphabet Inc. Google’s headquarters are located at the Googleplex in Mountain View, California, United States.

Market Position

Google Search and YouTube are among the most visited websites worldwide. Google is one of the most valuable and influential brands globally and is considered a major member of the "Big Tech" companies. The company has also faced criticism and regulatory scrutiny related to privacy, antitrust concerns, and market dominance.
