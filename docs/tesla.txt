Tesla most commonly refers to either the electric vehicle company Tesla, Inc. or the inventor Nikola Tesla. This document focuses on the primary meanings relevant to general knowledge and technology.

Tesla, Inc.

Tesla, Inc. is an American electric vehicle and clean energy company. It was founded in 2003 and is known for designing and manufacturing electric cars, battery energy storage systems, and solar energy products. The company was originally named Tesla Motors, Inc.

Tesla, Inc. is named after Nikola Tesla, an inventor and electrical engineer. The company is a major player in the electric vehicle market and has contributed significantly to the adoption of electric transportation worldwide.

Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla (1856–1943) was a Serbian-American electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and inventor. He is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system. Tesla’s work laid the foundation for many technologies used in electrical power generation and transmission.

Other Uses of the Term "Tesla"

The term "Tesla" may also refer to:

The tesla (T), an SI unit of magnetic flux density

Tesla, a former coal mining town in California

Various companies, media titles, scientific concepts, and places named after Nikola Tesla

Summary

The word "Tesla" is used in multiple contexts, most notably to describe Tesla, Inc., the electric vehicle company, and Nikola Tesla, the inventor. These meanings are commonly encountered in discussions related to technology, science, and industry.
